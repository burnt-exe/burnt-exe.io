IT Investigation Report

Investigation Summary

  • Investigation Title: Unauthorized Access and Data Breach Incident
  • Date of Incident: May 15, 2024
  • Date of Report: June 12, 2024
  • Investigator: Raydo Matthee
  • Incident Report Number: IRN-20240515-001

1. Incident Overview

On May 15, 2024, at approximately 10:00 AM, the IT department detected unusual activity on the company’s network. This activity was flagged by the Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) system, indicating a potential unauthorized access attempt. Immediate measures were taken to contain the incident, and an investigation was initiated to determine the scope and impact.

2. Objectives

  • Identify the root cause of the unauthorized access.
  • Determine the extent of the data breach.
  • Assess the impact on the organization’s data and systems.
  • Provide recommendations to prevent future incidents.

3. Methodology

  • Preparation: Gathered all relevant logs and data from SIEM, firewalls, and affected systems.
  • Identification: Identified compromised accounts and systems using digital forensics tools (EnCase, FTK).
  • Containment: Isolated affected systems to prevent further unauthorized access.
  • Eradication: Removed malware and unauthorized access points.
  • Recovery: Restored systems from backups and monitored for further suspicious activity.
  • Lessons Learned: Analyzed the incident to improve security measures.

4. Findings

  • Root Cause: The unauthorized access was traced to a compromised employee account, which was used to exploit a known vulnerability in the company’s VPN system.
  • Extent of Breach: Approximately 1,500 records containing sensitive employee information were accessed.
  • Impact: The breach compromised personal information, including names, addresses, and social security numbers. No financial information was accessed.

5. Digital Evidence

  • Logs: Detailed logs from the SIEM system showing the timeline of unauthorized access attempts.
  • Malware Analysis: Analysis of malware used in the attack, including its capabilities and origin.
  • Compromised Account: Forensic analysis of the compromised employee’s account activity.

6. Recommendations

Immediate Actions

  • Reset passwords for all compromised accounts.
  • Apply security patches to the VPN system.
  • Notify affected individuals and provide credit monitoring services.

Long-term Actions

  • Enhance employee training on phishing and social engineering attacks.
  • Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) for remote access.
  • Conduct regular security audits and vulnerability assessments.

7. Conclusion

The investigation revealed that the data breach was caused by a compromised employee account due to phishing. Immediate containment and eradication measures were effective in preventing further unauthorized access. Recommendations provided will enhance the organization’s security posture and mitigate future risks.
